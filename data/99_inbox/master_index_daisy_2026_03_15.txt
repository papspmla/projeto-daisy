INTRODUCTION
------------
This document consolidates the known historical monitoring of Daisy,
a female Rottweiler‑type dog under continuous home observation and
data‑driven clinical tracking by her tutor.

The monitoring program includes:

• Renal function surveillance
• Blood pressure monitoring
• Body temperature logging
• Weight tracking
• Urinary observation and culture history
• Stool morphology observation
• Diet and caloric management
• Musculoskeletal condition monitoring
• Estrous cycle observation
• Cardiac auscultation recordings

The aim of this document is to provide a structured clinical narrative
that can be read as a complete medical overview rather than a series of
isolated notes.

This file intentionally favours clarity and continuity over extreme
technical compression so that it can be easily shared with veterinarians
or researchers if necessary.

SECTION II – ACUTE KIDNEY INJURY EVENT (2025)
---------------------------------------------

In May 2025 Daisy experienced a clinically significant renal event
consistent with acute kidney injury (AKI).

Estimated timeline:

21/05/2025 – acute renal episode detected
11/06/2025 – beginning of laboratory recovery
31/10/2025 – stabilization phase confirmed

Subsequent laboratory interpretation indicates:

• creatinine returned to high‑normal range
• SDMA remained normal
• absence of significant proteinuria
• partial recovery of urinary concentration ability

Clinical conclusion:

Although the episode represented a serious renal insult, longitudinal
data strongly suggests that Daisy recovered meaningful renal function.


SECTION III – URINARY TRACT INFECTIONS
--------------------------------------

Daisy has a history of recurrent urinary infections requiring culture‑
guided antibiotic therapy.

Most recently identified pathogen:

Streptococcus canis

Treatment protocol:

Kesium (amoxicillin/clavulanate)
500/125 mg twice daily
Start: 16 January 2026
End: 31 January 2026
Total doses: 30

Management strategy emphasizes:

• culture confirmation
• targeted therapy
• avoidance of unnecessary antibiotics
• alignment with ISCAID 2019 guidelines

Compatible with slightly slow intestinal transit but within normal

SECTION X – CARDIAC AUSCULTATION
--------------------------------

Heart sounds have been recorded with a digital EKO device.

Interpretation rules used for Daisy recordings:

LLSB → mitral focus
APEX → tricuspid focus

To date no strong acoustic evidence of structural cardiac disease has
been documented.


SECTION XI – GLOBAL CLINICAL ASSESSMENT
---------------------------------------

Taking into account:

• renal recovery after AKI
• stable blood pressure
• absence of current bacteriuria
• maintained body weight
• normal behaviour and activity

Current overall assessment:

Daisy appears clinically stable with preserved physiological function
and no clear evidence of active systemic disease.

Continued structured monitoring remains advisable given the previous
renal episode and the history of urinary infections.
